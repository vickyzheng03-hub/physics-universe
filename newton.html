Newton's Laws

Newton's Laws explain how forces affect motion.

The three laws

  • First Law: An object stays at rest or keeps moving unless a force acts on it.
  • Second Law: Greater force causes greater acceleration. This is linked to F = m × a.
  • Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
Rocket pushes gas down
Rocket moves up

Everyday example

When you jump off the ground, your feet push down on the ground and the ground pushes you upward. That is an action-reaction pair.

Worked example

Question: A 4 kg object accelerates at 2 m/s². What is the net force?

Step 1: Use F = m × a.

Step 2: F = 4 × 2.

Step 3: F = 8 N.

Useful formula
F = m × a
  • Used for Newton's Second Law
  • Action and reaction are equal in size and opposite in direction

Mini quiz
