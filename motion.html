Motion

Motion describes how an object changes its position over time.

What is motion?

When an object moves from one place to another, it is in motion. To describe motion, students usually look at distance, time, and speed.

  • Distance tells us how far something travels.
  • Time tells us how long the movement takes.
  • Speed tells us how fast the object is moving.
Start
Finish

The longer the distance or the shorter the time, the speed changes.

Everyday example

A student walks 100 metres to school in 20 seconds during a sprint drill. That information can be used to find speed.

Worked example

Question: A bicycle travels 60 m in 12 s. What is its speed?

Step 1: Use the formula v = d ÷ t.

Step 2: Substitute the values: v = 60 ÷ 12.

Step 3: Calculate the answer: v = 5 m/s.

Main formula
v = d ÷ t
  • v = speed (m/s)
  • d = distance (m)
  • t = time (s)

Mini quiz

If a runner travels 40 m in 8 s, what is the speed?

Answer: 5 m/s, because 40 ÷ 8 = 5.
