Force

A force is a push or a pull that can change the motion of an object.

Key idea

Forces can make an object start moving, stop moving, speed up, slow down, or change direction.

  • Gravity pulls objects toward Earth.
  • Friction opposes motion between surfaces.
  • Applied force happens when a person or object pushes or pulls something.

Everyday example

When you push a shopping cart, your push is the applied force. Friction from the floor and wheels also affects the movement.

Worked example

Question: A 2 kg ball accelerates at 3 m/s². What force acts on it?

Step 1: Use F = m × a.

Step 2: Substitute the values: F = 2 × 3.

Step 3: F = 6 N.

Main formula
F = m × a
  • F = force (N)
  • m = mass (kg)
  • a = acceleration (m/s²)

Mini quiz

What is the unit of force?

Answer: The newton, written as N.
