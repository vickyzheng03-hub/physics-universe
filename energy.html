Energy

Energy is the ability to do work or cause change.

Types of energy

Energy can change from one form to another. For junior high school students, two important forms are kinetic energy and potential energy.

  • Kinetic energy is the energy of movement.
  • Potential energy is stored energy.
  • Energy is not created or destroyed. It changes form.

Everyday example

A moving bicycle has kinetic energy. A stretched rubber band stores elastic potential energy.

Worked example

Question: A 2 kg ball moves at 4 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?

Step 1: Use KE = 1/2mv².

Step 2: Substitute: KE = 1/2 × 2 × 4².

Step 3: KE = 16 J.

Main formula
KE = 1/2mv²
  • KE = kinetic energy (J)
  • m = mass (kg)
  • v = speed (m/s)

Mini quiz

Which type of energy does a moving car have?
